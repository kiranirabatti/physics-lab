The Human Eye

Class 10

What's going on?

Your cornea does most of the bending (about +40 D, fixed). The soft lens behind it does the fine-tuning: ciliary muscles squeeze it fatter (up to ~+24 D) for near things, and relax it thinner (~+20 D) for far things. A sharp image forms only if the rays meet exactly on the retina.

That's also why you can't read closer than about 25 cm — the lens simply can't get any fatter. And with age it stiffens, the near point drifts away, and grandparents hold the newspaper at arm's length — until convex reading glasses (the hypermetropia fix) bring it back.

Myopia = eyeball a bit too long → far things focus in front of the retina. Hypermetropia = a bit too short → near things focus behind it. Spectacles just add or remove a little bending power. Geometry, not magic.

Formula

P = 1/f(in m) dioptre
1/v − 1/u = 1/f
