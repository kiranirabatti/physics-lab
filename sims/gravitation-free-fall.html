Gravitation & Free Fall

Class 9

What's going on?

Gravity gives every object the same acceleration g — a cricket ball and a sheet of paper speed up identically. The paper only floats down slowly on Earth because air pushes back on its big flat surface, not because it is light.

And here's the trap every exam sets: mass is the amount of matter in an object — it never changes, anywhere in the universe. Weight is the force of gravity pulling on that mass, W = mg. Change the planet and the weight changes — the mass doesn't.

Formula

W = m × g  ·  v = g·t  ·  h = ½·g·t²

Try this: on Earth, turn air resistance ON and drop — the ball wins easily while the paper flutters at a steady ~2 m/s (be patient, real paper really is that slow — its trail dots become evenly spaced, meaning constant speed!). Now turn air OFF and drop again: they land at exactly the same moment. Then visit Jupiter — the ball's mass stays 0.16 kg but its weight jumps to almost 4 N.
