Electric Power & Heating

Class 10

What's going on?

Every appliance in your house gets the same push — 230 V from the mains. What differs is the current each one pulls: power P = V × I. A geyser gulps about 8.7 A; an LED bulb sips 0.04 A.

Anything whose job is heat — geyser, iron, kettle, room heater — needs huge power, because making heat (H = I²Rt) is expensive. That's why these few appliances decide your electricity bill, not the lights.

The meter counts units: 1 unit = 1 kWh. A geyser eats 2 units every hour. The LED would take almost 5 days of continuous running to use one unit.

Formula

P = V × I = I²R = V²/R
1 unit = 1 kWh  ·  cost = units × tariff
