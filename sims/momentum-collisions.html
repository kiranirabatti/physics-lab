Momentum & Collisions

Class 9

What's going on?

Momentum = mass × velocity. During the crash the carts push on each other with equal and opposite forces for exactly the same time (Newton's 3rd law) — so whatever momentum one cart loses, the other gains. The total can never change. Watch the momentum bar: the crash cannot budge it.

Kinetic energy plays by different rules. In an elastic collision it all survives. In a sticky (perfectly inelastic) one, part of it becomes heat, sound and crumpled metal — the red chunk of the energy bar.

Formula

m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
