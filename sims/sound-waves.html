Sound Waves

Class 9

What's going on?

Sound is not stuff flying to your ear — it's a vibration passing through air. The speaker pushes on nearby air particles; each particle just jiggles back and forth around its home spot (watch the coloured ones!), yet the pattern of squeezed bands (compressions) and stretched bands (rarefactions) races to the right at 343 m/s.

Frequency = vibrations per second = pitch: a tabla's loose skin vibrates slowly (low pitch), a whistle vibrates fast (high pitch). Amplitude = size of the jiggle = loudness — it changes the wave's height, never its spacing. The wave here is slowed about 100× so your eyes can follow it.

Formula

v = f × λ  ·  T = 1 / f
