Dispersion & Scattering

Class 10

What's going on?

Prism: glass slows violet light a little more than red (its refractive index is higher for violet). More slowing = more bending, at both faces. So white light fans out into VIBGYOR — violet bends most, red least. That's all dispersion is.

Sky: air molecules are tiny, so they scatter short (blue) waves far more than long (red) ones — about 10× more. At noon a little blue is knocked sideways into your eyes from every direction: blue sky. At sunset the light grazes through a much longer stretch of air, nearly all the blue is scattered away en route, and what survives is orange-red.

Same reason danger signs and brake lights are red: red scatters least, so it punches farthest through fog and dust.

Formula

scattering ∝ 1/λ⁴
nviolet (1.532) > nred (1.513)
