Work & Energy

Class 9

What's going on?

Lift the ball up the track and you do work against gravity — that work gets stored as potential energy (PE). Let go, and PE turns into kinetic energy (KE) as the ball speeds up. Going uphill, KE turns back into PE. The two keep trading places, but their total never changes.

It's exactly like a cycle rolling down a flyover slope without pedalling — height silently becomes speed. Turn friction ON and some energy escapes as heat on every pass: watch the heat bar collect exactly what PE and KE lose.

Formula

PE = mgh  ·  KE = ½mv²  ·  PE + KE = constant
