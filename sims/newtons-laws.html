Newton's Laws

Class 9

What's going on?

Ever pushed a loaded thela (hand-cart) and an empty one? Same push, but the empty cart leaps forward. That is Newton's 2nd law: acceleration = net force ÷ mass.

Friction is the spoiler. While the crate is at rest, static friction silently cancels your push — nothing moves until you beat μmg. Once it slides, kinetic friction keeps dragging it backwards.

Aristotle believed a force is needed just to keep something moving. Turn friction OFF and watch the crate glide forever with zero force — that is Newton's 1st law, and why a spacecraft never needs its engine on to coast.

Formula

Fnet = m × a
f = μN = μmg
