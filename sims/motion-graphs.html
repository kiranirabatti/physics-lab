Motion Graphs

Class 9

What's going on?

A motion graph is the story of a journey. Here the story belongs to an auto-rickshaw on a straight 100 m road.

In the s–t graph, height tells you where the auto is, and the steepness (slope) tells you how fast it moves. A flat line means it is parked. A line sloping down means it is coming back.

In the v–t graph, height is the velocity itself, and the shaded area the cursor sweeps out is the distance covered. Below the zero line means moving backwards.

Formula

v = Δs / Δt
slope of s–t = velocity
area under v–t = distance
