Mirror & Lens Formula

Class 10

What's going on?

The formula looks scary only because of the minus signs. The rule is simple: distances are measured from the mirror/lens, and anything on the left (the side the light comes from) gets a minus. That's why the object distance is always written u = −30 cm, never +30.

Put the signed numbers in, and the formula tells you where the image is: v negative → image on the left; v positive → image on the right. Negative m → inverted image. No memorising cases — the signs do the thinking.

Try dragging the object arrow in the diagram — the equation and picture always agree.

Formula

mirror: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f,  m = −v/u
lens:  1/v − 1/u = 1/f,  m = v/u
h′ = m·h,  P = 100/f(cm) D
